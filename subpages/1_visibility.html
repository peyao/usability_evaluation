1. Visibility

Do you know where you are when navigating or performing an action or transaction? “The system should always keep users informed about what is going on, through appropriate feedback within reasonable time.” The University of Leicester website does a decent job at informing the user know where they are in terms of navigation and feedback. They have an interesting take on the bare minimum of a logo and navigation bar, as one can see in the homepage: the navigation bar is neither at the top or side of the page, rather it is situated in the middle. The logo is consistently placed in the top left of any page one visits which is the least they can do. The layout of their pages are similar but only at the 2nd child level. Once one reaches the 2nd level site, there’s no breadcrumbs back to the previous page, for example as seen on the “Maps and Directions” page there is only an exit back to the homepage not the previous page. The navigation bar succeeds in visually displaying to the user which page is currently active. This is done by the section being hilighted as a gray box and there is a title at the top of the page. Both the section on the navigation bar and the title are worded in the exact manner. The main links on the homepage (“Study with Us”, “Our Research”, “Business & Enterprise”, and “Alumni & Supporters”) have extended menu drop down options. But it does not do that even further within that drop down selections which is a bit deceiving because there are arrows at the end of each selection.
